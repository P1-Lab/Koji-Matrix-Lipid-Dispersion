# FAT-OS: Koji-Matrix Lipid Dispersion (KMLD) Protocol

### **Technical Overview**
**FAT-OS** is an open-source technical framework for the creation of shelf-stable, programmable plant-based lipids. By utilizing a multi-layered vitrification process, this protocol addresses the "behavioral gap" in plant-based food systems—mimicking the slow-release succulence and elevated smoke points of rendered animal fats without oxidation or premature leakage.

---

### **The Problem: Physical Wall Failure**
Standard plant-based oils lack structural integrity during thermal transitions. **FAT-OS** provides a biological programming layer that "traps" lipids within a fermented chitin/glucan matrix, releasing them only when specific environmental triggers (thermal, hydration, or shear) are met.

---

### **Core Standards**
To achieve **Vitrification Grade** status, all implementations must adhere to the following technical constraints:
*   **Moisture Content:** $\le$ 2.0% via controlled dehydration to reach a glassy state.
*   **Oxidative Stability:** Minimum 24-month shelf life via nitrogen-flushed packaging.
*   **Encapsulation Layering:** A primary koji-fermented mushroom matrix for umami-cation binding, followed by a secondary starch-vitrification layer (Yam or Jicama).

---

### **Sequential Release Architecture**
The system operates on three discrete triggers:
1.  **Thermal:** The starch lattice crosses the glass transition temperature ($T_g$) at 60°C–120°C, initiating a controlled render.
2.  **Hydration:** Plasticization of the outer starch barrier upon contact with water for instant dispersion.
3.  **Mechanical (Shear):** Physical fracture of the vitrified wall for immediate flavor release during mastication or aerosolization.
