# AUDIT-FRAMEWORK.md: Quality Assurance Standards

This document establishes the clinical audit protocols required to verify **Vitrification Grade** integrity for Koji-Matrix Lipid Dispersion (KMLD). Adherence to these standards is mandatory for all licensed implementation partners to ensure consistent behavioral performance of the "Fat OS".

---

## 1. Structural Integrity Audits
The following forensic tests verify that the "Physical Walls" of the lipid matrix are intact and compliant with the technical specification.

### 1.1 Lattice Vitrification Check
*   **Test Method:** Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC).
*   **Success Metric:** The sample must exhibit a clear glass transition ($T_g$) baseline above $55^\circ\text{C}$ in its dry state.
*   **Failure Mode:** Any exothermic or endothermic peaks below $50^\circ\text{C}$ indicate incomplete dehydration or barrier contamination.

### 1.2 Lipid Migration (Sequestration) Test
*   **Test Method:** The "White Paper" Incubation.
*   **Procedure:** Place 50g of KMLD powder on high-absorbency chromatography paper in a $45^\circ\text{C}$ incubator for 72 hours.
*   **Success Metric:** Zero visible oil staining on the substrate (Total Sequestration).
*   **Failure Mode:** Visible oil leaching indicates a failure in the starch-vitrification shield or improper lipid-to-scaffold ratios.

## 2. Chemical & Stability Benchmarks
Quantitative verification of the 24-month shelf-stability claim.

### 2.1 Moisture Precision
*   **Standard:** $\le 2.0\%$ total moisture content by weight.
*   **Equipment:** Halogen moisture analyzer or vacuum oven drying.
*   **Frequency:** Every production lot.

### 2.2 Oxidative Resistance
*   **Standard:** Peroxide Value (PV) $\le 1.0\text{ meq/kg}$ post-vitrification.
*   **Standard:** Anisidine Value (AV) $\le 3.0$.
*   **Audit Protocol:** Samples must be nitrogen-flushed and tested at T-0 and T-12 months.

## 4. Compliance & Certification
*   **Level 1 (Standard):** Self-audit via this framework; suitable for general MIT-licensed use.
*   **Level 2 (Certified):** Forensic audit conducted by **Chart Atlas** or authorized delegates; required for commercial "Standard Food Primary" status.
*   **Revocation:** Failure to meet the $\le 2.0\%$ moisture standard or PV limits results in immediate loss of Vitrification Grade certification.
