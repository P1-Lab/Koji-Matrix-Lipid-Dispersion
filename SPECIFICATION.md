# SPECIFICATION.md: Core Technical Standard for KMLD

This document defines the mechanical requirements and physical constraints for achieving **Vitrification Grade** in Koji-Matrix Lipid Dispersion (KMLD). 

---

## 1. Physical Wall Architecture
The KMLD system is composed of a dual-layered structure designed to prevent lipid coalescence and oxidative rancidity.

### 1.1 The Primary Matrix (Biological Scaffold)
*   **Substrate:** Koji-fermented mushroom (chitin/beta-glucan scaffold).
*   **Function:** Acts as a microscopic sponge to trap lipid droplets through surface tension and porosity modification.
*   **Enzymatic Baseline:** *Aspergillus oryzae* must be utilized to achieve the necessary pore-size distribution for lipid retention.

### 1.2 The Secondary Barrier (Vitrified Shield)
*   **Substrate:** High-amylose yam or jicama starches.
*   **Function:** Formation of a protective glassy lattice that halts molecular mobility.

---

## 2. Vitrification Parameters
To ensure a shelf life of 24 months, the following thresholds are mandatory:

*   **Final Moisture Content:** $\le$ 2.0%.
*   **Atmospheric Control:** Nitrogen flushing to displace oxygen prior to final sealing.
*   **Glass Transition ($T_g$):** The material must remain in a glassy state at standard ambient temperatures ($< 25^\circ\text{C}$).

---

## 3. Phase Change Release Mechanics
Release is triggered by forcing the vitrified shield into a rubbery state or physical failure.

| Trigger Type | Threshold | Mechanical Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Thermal** | $60^\circ\text{C} - 120^\circ\text{C}$ | Starch lattice crosses $T_g$; controlled lipid "render". |
| **Hydration** | $a_w > 0.85$ | Plasticization of the starch barrier; instant dispersion. |
| **Mechanical** | $> 15 \text{ N/cm}^2$ | Brittle fracture of the vitrified wall (mastication/aerosol). |

---

## 4. Audit & Verification
Standardized audits must verify lattice integrity.
*   **Stability Test:** 48-hour incubation at $40^\circ\text{C}$ with zero lipid "weeping".
*   **Purity Check:** Absence of off-flavors derived from lipid oxidation post-dehydration.
